About the Group

The Biotechnology and Natural Products Research Group develops experimental research at the interface of phytochemistry, analytical chemistry, plant biotechnology, and biomass valorization. Our work focuses on plant-derived specialized metabolites, particularly pentacyclic triterpene acids from Cecropia species, and their applications in health-related and bioeconomy-oriented research.

We integrate chromatographic analysis, metabolomics, and experimental phytochemistry to understand biosynthetic pathways, characterize complex botanical materials, and support the development of sustainable plant-based bioproducts.

Research Lines

  • Natural products discovery and phytochemical characterization
  • Pentacyclic triterpene acids from Cecropia angustifolia
  • Plant biotechnology and in-vitro biomass production
  • Metabolomics and chromatographic analysis of plant materials
  • Biomass valorization within bioeconomy frameworks
